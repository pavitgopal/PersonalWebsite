Undergraduate Research

TAMU SpaceTeams Research Lab

In Dr. Chamitoff's research lab, I leveraged Unreal Engine, C++, and object-oriented programming principles to develop an algorithm for enhancing terrain rendering within existing VR simulations. This project seamlessly integrated aerophysics, visual graphics, and computer science to optimize the geometric modeling of celestial bodies.

Through the utilization of array manipulation, bilinear interpolation, and weight height calculations in space, we successfully created a cone-shaped distribution of terrain on computer-generated planetary surfaces. The most significant challenge we encountered during this project centered on the implementation of our algorithm. To address this challenge, we harnessed Unreal Engine's line tracing capabilities to convert our data points from a planet-centered coordinate system to longitude and latitude coordinates.

UNT IEMR Research Lab

Worked in Dr.Chyan's Interfacial and elecrotchemical research lab to characterize electrochemical depositions on Copper, Silver, and Vanadium wafers through infrared spectroscopy (IR). As an undergraduate researcher, I was responsible for running electrochemistry experiments, analyzing IR signatures, and performing sample etching.

Additionally, I furthered the lab's wafer analysis process by developing a Python application which computerizes the testing of copper wafers. Through binary subtraction, the program determines the efficacy of various samples by comparing images pre and post HCL etching.

In addition, I had the opportunity to present my findings at UNT Scholars Day, receiving two highly competitive scholarships totaling $4,500. Link to Scholar's Day research poster can be found here.
